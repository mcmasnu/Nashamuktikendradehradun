Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is admission confidential?

Yes, completely. We take privacy very seriously. Patient identity and treatment details are never disclosed without explicit consent. You can seek help without fear of social or professional consequences.

How long does treatment take?

Treatment duration depends on the individual, the substance involved, and the severity of dependency. Programs typically range from 30 days to 6 months. We will provide a personalised recommendation after assessment.

Can family members visit during treatment?

Yes, family visits are encouraged at appropriate stages of treatment. We also involve families through counselling sessions to support the recovery process. Our team will guide you on the visiting schedule.

What does treatment cost?

We offer flexible, affordable packages without compromising on quality. Costs depend on the program type and duration. Please contact us for a detailed, transparent cost breakdown — there are no hidden charges.

Can I admit someone who doesn't want help?

We understand that many individuals resist help at first. Our counsellors can advise families on how to approach this sensitively. Voluntary treatment generally has better outcomes, and we can guide families through the conversation.

Do you offer outpatient treatment?

Yes, in some cases, outpatient counselling and follow-up sessions are available. Contact us to discuss whether an outpatient arrangement is suitable for your situation.
